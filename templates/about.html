About Career Navigator

Problem Statement

Choosing a career path is a critical decision for students, often influenced by a complex interplay of personal, academic, and financial factors. Many students lack access to personalized guidance that considers their unique profiles, including academic performance, personality traits, interests, and financial constraints. Traditional career counseling methods may not scale effectively or leverage data-driven insights, leading to suboptimal career choices that do not align with individual strengths or market demands.

Solution

The Career Navigator is a web-based application that uses machine learning to provide personalized career recommendations. By collecting data on users' age, education level, academic scores, personality traits, study habits, extracurricular activities, hobbies, interests, budget, and career demand preferences, the system predicts suitable career paths. The model, trained on a comprehensive dataset, employs a Random Forest Classifier to ensure robust predictions. The user-friendly interface allows students to input their details through an interactive form, receiving tailored career suggestions that align with their profiles and aspirations.

Architecture Diagram

The Career Navigator follows a client-server architecture. The frontend, built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, uses Bootstrap for responsive design and Flask's Jinja2 templating for dynamic content. The backend, powered by Flask, handles form submissions, preprocesses data using StandardScaler, and makes predictions with a pre-trained Random Forest model. The model is trained on a synthetic dataset and serialized using joblib for efficient loading. The diagram below illustrates the data flow from user input to career prediction.

Dataset Source and Features

The Career Navigator uses a synthetic dataset, icn_dataset_30k.csv, sourced from Kaggle. This dataset contains 30,000 records simulating student profiles and their corresponding career outcomes. The features, listed below, capture a wide range of attributes to ensure accurate career predictions. The dataset is preprocessed using StandardScaler to normalize numerical features before training the Random Forest model.

Source: Kaggle - Synthetic Career Dataset

Feature Description
Age User's age (15-70)
Education Level Highest education level (Grade 10, Grade 11, Grade 12, UG, PG)
CGPA Cumulative Grade Point Average (0.0-10.0)
Math Score Math performance score (0-100)
Physics Score Physics performance score (0-100)
Biology Score Biology performance score (0-100)
History Score History performance score (0-100)
Openness Personality trait: openness to new experiences (1-10)
Conscientiousness Personality trait: organization and dependability (1-10)
Extraversion Personality trait: outgoing and social nature (1-10)
Agreeableness Personality trait: compassion and cooperation (1-10)
Neuroticism Personality trait: emotional instability (1-10)
Weekly Self-Study Hours Hours spent on self-study per week (0-100)
Extracurricular Activities Participation in extracurricular activities (0: No, 1: Yes)
Hobbies User-selected hobbies (e.g., coding, cooking, dance)
Interests Primary interest area (arts, business, healthcare, technology)
Budget Education/training budget in lakhs INR (1-20)
Career Demand Score Perceived job market demand (1-10)
